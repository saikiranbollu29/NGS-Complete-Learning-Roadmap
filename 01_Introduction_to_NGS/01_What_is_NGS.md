# 🧬 What is Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)?

## 📖 Introduction

DNA contains the genetic instructions required for the growth, development, functioning, and reproduction of all living organisms. To understand these genetic instructions, scientists determine the exact order of nucleotides (A, T, G, and C) in DNA through a process called **DNA sequencing**.

Initially, DNA sequencing was performed using **Sanger Sequencing**, which was highly accurate but slow, expensive, and capable of sequencing only one DNA fragment at a time. As biological research expanded and entire genomes needed to be analyzed, a faster and more scalable technology became necessary.

This led to the development of **Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)**, also known as **High-Throughput Sequencing (HTS)**, which revolutionized genomics by enabling millions to billions of DNA fragments to be sequenced simultaneously.

---

# 📚 What is DNA Sequencing?

**DNA sequencing** is the process of determining the exact order of nucleotides (A, Adenine; T, Thymine; G, Guanine; and C, Cytosine) in a DNA molecule.

For example,

```
DNA Sequence

ATGCTAGCTAGCTAGCTA
```

DNA sequencing tells us:

- Which genes are present.
- Whether mutations exist.
- Genetic differences between individuals.
- Causes of inherited diseases.
- Evolutionary relationships.

---

# 🧬 What is Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)?

**Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)** is a high-throughput DNA sequencing technology that allows millions to billions of DNA or RNA fragments to be sequenced simultaneously in a single experiment.

Unlike Sanger sequencing, which sequences one DNA fragment at a time, NGS performs **massively parallel sequencing**, making it much faster, more cost-effective, and suitable for large-scale genomic studies.

NGS is widely used in genomics research, clinical diagnostics, cancer genomics, infectious disease surveillance, precision medicine, agriculture, microbiome research, and drug discovery.

---

# 💡 Why Was NGS Developed?

Scientists wanted to overcome the limitations of Sanger sequencing.

Problems with Sanger sequencing included:

- Slow sequencing speed.
- High cost for large genomes.
- Low throughput.
- Not suitable for sequencing millions of DNA fragments.
- Difficult to analyze complex genomes efficiently.

NGS solved these problems by sequencing millions of DNA fragments in parallel, dramatically increasing speed while reducing cost per base.

---

# ⚙ Key Principle of NGS

The fundamental principle of NGS is **Massively Parallel Sequencing**.

Instead of sequencing one DNA fragment,

NGS sequences

```
Fragment 1

Fragment 2

Fragment 3

Fragment 4

...

Millions of fragments simultaneously
```

These millions of short sequencing reads are then assembled or aligned to a reference genome using bioinformatics tools.

# 🏥 Applications of NGS

NGS has transformed both research and clinical medicine.

Some major applications include:

- Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS)
- Whole Exome Sequencing (WES)
- RNA Sequencing (RNA-Seq)
- Cancer Genomics
- Clinical Diagnostics
- Rare Disease Identification
- Pharmacogenomics
- Infectious Disease Detection
- Metagenomics
- Precision Medicine
- Agricultural Genomics
- Evolutionary Biology

---

# ✅ Advantages of NGS

- Extremely high throughput.
- Faster than Sanger sequencing.
- Lower cost per base.
- Detects multiple types of genetic variants.
- Suitable for whole-genome analysis.
- Highly scalable.
- Supports a wide range of applications.

---

# ❌ Limitations of NGS

- High initial instrument cost.
- Generates very large datasets.
- Requires bioinformatics expertise.
- Complex data analysis.
- Storage and computational resources are needed.
- Some platforms have platform-specific biases.
